Case Study: Note Apps

An in-depth case study of what makes a good desktop note app by first asking the question of how people write things down using keyboard instead of pen.

  • Works
  • About

Background/ Design Problem

I am a big fan of note taking through out my years in college and now in work, and after using quite a few different notes/office production apps. I had some experience and ideas that i want to share with you all.

Goal

I chose three notes/wiki apps, Apple Note, Evernote and Notion which i have all used for quite a long time to do a in-depth case study of what makes a good note app by first asking the question of how people nowadays write things down using keyboard instead of pen.

User Research and Insights

I started observe myself taking notes and trying to understand what' s the first important things for me when i m note taking.

I find myself normally went through a workflow of:

  • get familiar with the menu >
    • no sneak peak on the content, because sometimes they are sensitive info
    • quick search allow you to find you note even faster
    • quick access to specific notes
  • open or delete a page >
  • working on the writing >
  • editing here and there (involving text styling (type, font size&color)/mark hierarchies/ sorting text sections/ coloring and highlighting) >
    • editing features happens in hover vs. Evernote editing features attached.
  • finish writing and trying to 1.save it to some where i can find 2.publish to other medium, 3.download or print >
  • pray it never changes or disappear >
  • if working in team, one needs options to sync working regularly and see changes in history.
    • great collaborative mode

and the above answer the very question of what are the most important features a good note kind app should tackle.

Show and Tell

So then a good note app needs to have:

  1. clear info hiehierarchy structure of where a finished page goes.























  2. a prominent way to open a new note page and delete one
    • Evernote and Apple Note have an add button on the top menu where a new page is automatic added to the current folder.Notion on the other way place add button on the side menu direct under folders/pages.











    • I think its very much needed to give user option to add page direct inside the side bar, so we know instantly where a page is in the hierarch and its relationship to the parent and sibling folders. Also when later users wish to replace it, the drag is much simpler and faster.








  3. Text font, size, color and alignment.
    • Evernote - top bar


















    • Notion - only 3 fonts and 2 sizes to choose from















  4. Selecting between different tags
    • Evernote








    • Apple











    • Notion - 2 ways: by typing a "/" to choose or select the block to choose










  5. Text-attribute
    • Evernote does but in a old fashion way. User need to preselect text they wish to change and then choose from the option bar above.






    • Apple doesn't support any direct menu bar to change text attributes but only keyboard shortcut or right click on the text.












    • Notion allows user to change the text attribute right next to the cursor. Very consistent and non-brainer.










  6. Sync and version tracks for collaborative works.
  7. Publish/Export/Download/Print
